Progress Report


Everyday twenty-two people die waiting for a transplant. Around 65% of organs are rejected by centers while 10% of accepted organs are not used in transplants. Rejections occur due to low confidence from doctors about compatibility, time spent waiting for decisions being made, and human error. This $34B industry is missing many chances at saving lives and money. UNOS stores data for the thousands of transplants that occur and current medical charts have hundreds of data points on patients. This seemed like a perfect opportunity to let machines solve this problem.

Dataset Details


The data we yielded from is here.

It contains category of the following:

id - The id of each anonymous individual.

dr -

age - Each person's age.

blood - Each person's blood type.

gender - Each person's gender.

organ - The type of organ is being transplanted. Category is Kidney, Heart, Pancrea, Liver, and Lung

ethnicity - Their ethnicity. Category is White, Asian, Black, and Hispanic

bmi - Individual's BMI (Body Mass Index) which is a good indicator of their state of health. Category is in the range of [17, 33]

acceptance - If the organ is accepted. Category is True or False
